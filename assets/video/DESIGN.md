# Agent4 Intro Video Design

## Style Prompt

A restrained technical-documentary motion piece for an AI engineering workflow. The screen should feel like a verified handoff console: dark navy canvas, precise contract lines, warm amber risk markers, mint verification signals, and sparse cyan structural paths. The tone is audit-ready rather than promotional.

## Colors

- Background ink: `#07111d`
- Panel navy: `#0d2138`
- Primary text: `#f4f0e6`
- Secondary text: `#a8d8e8`
- Contract cyan: `#22c7e7`
- Verification mint: `#20d6a0`
- Risk amber: `#ff9d2e`
- Fail red: `#e84646`

## Typography

- Display/body voice: `Agent4 Serif` backed by a bundled subset WOFF2, serif for Chinese statements and compact explanations.
- Technical/data voice: `IBM Plex Mono`, monospace for schema IDs, commands, and artifact labels.

## Motion

- Medium-energy technical pacing with directional wipes and block sweeps.
- Entrances should vary: line draws, masked panels, scale snaps, x/y drifts, and terminal-like reveals.
- No jump cuts. Scene changes use a shared contract-gate wipe so the video feels like one verification pipeline.
- Ambient motion remains subtle: slow grid drift, small glow shifts, and diagram pans.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use generic purple/blue gradient branding.
- Do not rely on full architecture diagrams as unreadable whole-screen posters.
- Do not make the video feel like a landing page hero.
- Do not introduce colors outside the contract/risk/verification palette.
- Do not imply Agent4 bypasses Agent1 regression or Agent5 handoff.
